The 2-Minute Rule Is Wrong. Here's What Actually Works.
By Freddy Owen  ·  4 min read
This Week's Tip

You've heard the 2-minute rule: if something takes less than 2 minutes, do it now. It's fine advice. But it misses the real problem - which isn't that tasks are too long. It's that starting feels terrible.

The fix: Instead of a time threshold, use an entry threshold. Ask yourself, "What is the smallest possible first action?" Not "finish the report" - "open the doc." Not "work out" - "put on shoes." The bar is: can you do this in 30 seconds with zero willpower? If yes, that's your start.

Once you're in motion, momentum does the rest. The science on this is solid - it's called the Zeigarnik effect. Your brain hates unfinished things and will push you to complete them once started. Use it.
